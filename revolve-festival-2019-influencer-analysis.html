by Lore Veltri

With the raise of social media influencers, Revolve as many e-retailers, is partnering with talent creators in lieu of investing in traditional advertising which seems to have a low impact on their Millennials and Gen Z consumers.

Content creators and talents whom Revolve work with are responsible for the majority of the site's revenue.

According to Michael Mente, Co-Founder and Co-Chief Executive Officer of Revolve, “up to 70 percent of the site’s revenues are fueled by influencers.”

In 2017, “the privately-owned company reported $1 billion in sales—an accomplishment that it attributes, in large part ($650-$700 million), to their network of influencers and the ground-level marketing tactics at events like Coachella.”

Key questions answered in this insight report include:

  • Who are the influencers involved into Revolve’s instagram strategy?

  • How Revolve selected the influencers for the Coachella campaign 2019?

  • What is working right now on Influencer Marketing for Revolve?
